Bring NPLP into Mass Production.

Nihon Denji Sokki is working with Dr. Masato Sagawa to commercialize his neodymium magnet molding system. This new pressless process (NPLP) is a Near Net Shape (NNS) production technology that offers advantages over other sintered material processes when fabricating magnets with complex shapes for use in motors or other related equipment.

NPLP Magnets

On Going Research

Although advances in traditional molding processes such as Axial Die Pressing (ADP) or Transverse Die Pressing (TDP) have reduced average particle diameters used in the mold down to 3.8 μm, these technologies seem to have reached their limit. NPLP aims for sizes near 1 μm, while addressing issues with scarcity and high price of rare earths by eliminating to need for dysprosium (Dy). Also, we see opportunities for improvements in coercivities. While TDP methods offer coercivities of 1240 ~ 1600 kA /m., our ongoing research into these new methods aims to achieve coercivities approaching 2400 kA/m.

Industrial Neodymium Magnets

To sinter magnetic powders into shapes that are as near as possible to the optimal design for motor efficiency, the uniformity of the powder packing is critical when the mold is being filled. Here is where NPLP enjoys an advantage over other methods thanks to its use of long narrow molds. NPLP can achieve a fill factor of 3.5 g/cc which yields some of the most highly packed magnets with densities of 7.52 g/cc.

In order to achieve good results using ultrafine powder with diameters of 1.1μm, the mold is set into a closed environment with oxygen content lower than 0.0001%. Molds are filled at densities of 3.2-3.6g/cm using an air tap (AT) process. The molds are then sintered as a pulsed external field of 5 Tesla is applied.

When producing magnets, the data shows that coercivity and grain sizes are inversely correlated. TDP methods use materials where grains average 5 μm in diameter and this yields a typical coercivity of about 1040 kA/m. But pressless process methods, which are capable of processing powders with average grain sizes of 1.2 μm, can achieve 1600 kA /m.
