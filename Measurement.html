Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC) System

Our industrial magnet presses use a timed, floating-fill method that is run by a programmable logic controller (PLC). This system ensures a uniform fill rate that minimizes any shrinkage to the sintered magnet block. This prevents any deformation from occurring.

While the magnet presses are running through their compression cycle, hydraulic pressures and flow rates must be monitored and controlled precisely. This function is done by our PLC System, which adjusts the pressure at the valves automatically based on feedback obtained throughout the pressing process.

This level of control allows us to use high-precision molds with single-sided gaps of 25μ m. This level of accuracy of the mold helps limit issues related to powder leakage. Leakage will not only waste material but will also leave traces on the magnet block called flash. These blocks will then need to be reworked by the operators to remove it. Therefore, PLC ability to eliminate flash results in saving on time and labor cost. Leakage prevents also protect the production environment from dust containment.

Control of the Coil Assembly

The PLC must ensure that the coil is always working at a constant temperature. This is accomplished by balancing the magnetizer’s power supply and the coil’s cooling system. Controlling the temperature not only stabilizes magnetic field but also greatly reduces costs due to electrical consumption.

Network System Monitoring

Our PLC system has a built-in communication module that allows you to interface our presses with your factory’s network to monitor its operation remotely over the Internet.

The PLC has a touch screen interface for easy setup. It is convenient and offers simple operation and quick installation.
